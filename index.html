Understanding

Earth Surface Temperature

I am Duo CHAN, a 5th-year Ph.D. student working with Peter Huybers at the Department of Earth and Planetary Sciences, Harvard University. My research focuses on developing statistical and physical tools for reconstructing past climate and understanding climate variability, with a particular interest in surface temperature. My recent works include recovering sea surface temperature (SST) since the 1850s and understanding land temperature variability in climate models.

RESEARCH HIGHLIGHTS

SST Reconstruction

In addition to accounting for the cold bucket bias and the warm engine room intake bias, can we improve the correction for historical sea surface temperature (SST) records and increase the accuracy of global warming estimates?

Learn More

Land Temperature Variability

How much more variable will summer over the extra-tropical continent become? The answer varies dramatically from model to model. Can we understand why models behave differently and improve the confidence of projections?
