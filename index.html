Climate and Data Science

I am a climate scientist and a lecturer (equivelant to Assistant Professor) in the school of Ocean and Earth Sciences at Univeristy of Southampton, UK. Before that, I was a Postdoc Scholar at WHOI and a Ph.D. student at Harvard University.

I develop statistical and physical tools to quantify and understand the mechanisms of climate change. I have a broad interest, including but not limited to:

(1) Climate data and statistical climatology

(2) Machine learning in climate and environmental studies

(3) Climate variability and climate dynamics

(4) Large-scale air-sea and land-air Interaction

Recruiting PhD students

I am recruiting a PhD student through the INSPIRE PhD training project. Successful candidata will have a full fellowship for at least three years and will conduct research on long-term changes in the equitorial Pacific climate. For details about the project, please search for Duo Chan on the INSPIRE project page.

Application Requirements:

    (1) A strong interest in climate change,

    (2) A solid background in mathematics and sciences, with a Master’s/Bachelor’s degree obtained or soon to be obtained in mathematics, physics, computer science, atmospheric or oceanic sciences,

    (3) Proficiency in English communication and academic skills.

Interested candidates are welcome to contact me directly.
