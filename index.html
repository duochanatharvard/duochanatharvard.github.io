Understanding

Earth's Temperature Variations

I am a climate scientist, and I develop statistical and physical tools to understand how the Earthâ€™s climate works, with the ultimate goal of making more accurate climate projections. I am a Postdoc Scholar at WHOI after obtaining my Ph.D. at Harvard University in 2021.

I led the group-wise correction of historical sea-surface temperatures. My recent work involves using the physics of air-sea coupling to homogenize historical records of land and ocean surface temperatures.

Other ongoing work involves: (1) using the dynamics of ocean circulation to study ocean heat uptake throughout the past 20k years; (2) using machine-learning methods to perform data assimilation for early observations and paleo proxies.

I am also interested in a variety of topics, including ocean circulation, air-sea coupling and implications for hurricane genesis and atmospheric jets, and land-air interaction and implications for continental heatwaves and crop production.
